Reactance is the opposition to current flow caused by capacitors and inductors in an AC circuit, measured in **ohms (Ω)**. Unlike resistance, reactance does not dissipate energy but stores and releases it. Capacitive reactance decreases with frequency, while inductive reactance increases with frequency.
