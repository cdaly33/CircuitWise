Resistance is the opposition to the flow of electrical current, measured in **ohms (Ω)**, named after Georg Ohm. Every material has some resistance — conductors like copper have very low resistance, while insulators like rubber have extremely high resistance. Resistance depends on a material's composition, length, thickness, and temperature. It converts electrical energy into heat, which is how devices like light bulbs, toasters, and heaters work.
