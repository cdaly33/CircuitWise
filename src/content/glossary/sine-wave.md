A sine wave is a smooth, repeating mathematical curve that describes how AC voltage changes over time. It starts at zero, rises to a positive peak, returns through zero to a negative peak, and then comes back to zero to complete one cycle. The shape is fundamental to understanding how alternating current behaves.
