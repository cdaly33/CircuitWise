## AC vs DC: The Basics

There are two fundamental types of electrical current: **alternating current (AC)** and **direct current (DC)**.

### Direct Current (DC)

Direct current flows in one direction only. Batteries produce DC power.

### Alternating Current (AC)

Alternating current reverses direction many times per second. The power in your wall outlets is AC.

### Why Both Exist

AC is efficient for long-distance power transmission, while DC is better for electronics and battery-powered devices.
